WORK

For the American Lawyer I created a Sankey chart in Tableau that showed the flow of diversity into the types of lawyers and within law firms. The data was generated by a survey I sent out over a period of two months. The chart was fully interactive with the user having the ability to add up 160 of the largest 200 law firms in the country.

For Corporate Counsel's online magazine I created an infographic that displayed the top law firms under categories such as buyouts, various types of debt, and equities. The data was culled from various outside vendors. A ranking chart was created as well to showcase how firms moved from 2013 - 2014.

This dashboard was used to track the Econonomic Confidence Index. Rather than send the client a new powerpoint every three months with the updated numbers, I created a Tableau Server graph that only the client had access to. Every three months I would update the numbers. This was also helpful as the client could access the report at anypoint and on any device.

This was a proposed dashboard as an add-on a larger report on the statiscs of a Law Firm's Mid-level Associates. In conjecture with an overview of the survey, this tool would allow a law firm to pull statistics at a moment's whim. It was also helpful on our end as we could generate one report and then automatically generate each custom report at the press of a button. This project was well received by the Chief Technology Officer but sadly it has been not pushed into field. It is still under consideration.
